Forecasting UK Whisky & Gin Exports Under Tariffs and FTAs

🎯 Executive Summary

This project builds an end-to-end forecasting framework for UK whisky and gin exports to the United States and India, linking monthly HMRC CN8 trade flows with GDP, CPIH spirits inflation, and GBP exchange rates.

By comparing SARIMAX with gradient-boosted XGBoost models, I quantify both the historical impact of U.S. tariffs (2019–2021) and prospective gains or downside under alternative UK–India FTA scenarios, then expose the results through an interactive dashboard designed for policymakers and distillery decision-makers.

💼 The Business Problem

UK distilleries and trade negotiators are making multi-year capacity and pricing decisions while whisky exports respond month-by-month to:

  • Tariff shocks - Sudden trade barriers that can wipe out margins overnight
  • Exchange-rate swings - GBP volatility affecting competitiveness in key markets
  • Local economic cycles - GDP and inflation driving demand for premium spirits

Without a forward-looking, scenario-based view, producers risk over-producing into depressed demand when tariffs spike, or under-serving key markets like India at the exact moment when an FTA unlocks new volume.

🔍 Key Strategic Questions

  • How sensitive are UK whisky and gin exports to macro-variables like CPIH, GDP and FX?
  • What would UK exports have looked like without U.S. tariffs?
  • How much upside is on the table under specific UK–India FTA configurations?

💡 The "So What?" - Business Impact

  • Market Expansion Strategy: By mathematically linking consumer price index (CPIH) and exchange rate volatility to export demand, distilleries can dynamically route inventory to whichever market (US vs. India) presents the most favorable economic conditions that quarter.
  • Risk Mitigation: Quantifies how resilient the product category is to global inflation, enabling better hedging and pricing strategy.
  • Policy Influence: Provides negotiators with data-driven estimates of export gains under specific FTA pathways, strengthening the UK's position in trade talks.
  • Strategic Agility: Enabled inventory routing based on real-time GBP–INR currency fluctuations.

🔧 Data & Methodology

Data Sources

  • HMRC Overseas Trade Statistics (OTGS): Monthly UK exports by CN8, filtered to whisky and gin (HS2 22, HS4 2208, CN8 codes 22083071/79, 22083082)
  • ONS CPIH Spirits Index: Tracking real price movements in domestic spirits inflation
  • IMF World Economic Outlook: GDP series for U.S. and India as demand-side income proxies
  • Bank of England: Daily spot exchange rates aggregated to monthly GBP–USD and GBP–INR indices

Data Engineering

  • Harmonized HMRC exports across multiple whisky and gin CN8 codes into a single panel, preserving partner-country, month and HS classification
  • Cleaned and resampled GDP, CPIH spirits and FX data to consistent monthly frequency (2015–2025)
  • Merged all indicators into merged_uk_exports_all_data.csv at country-month level

Modelling Approach

  • Train/Test Split: Training (2015–2022), Testing (2023–2025) to mimic realistic forecasting horizon
  • Model Comparison: SARIMAX (autoregressive + exogenous drivers) vs. XGBoost (non-linear gradients) on MAE, RMSE, MAPE
  • Final Selection: XGBoost outperformed SARIMAX on test-period volumes

Scenario Analysis

  • No-Tariff Counterfactual: Set tariff-period indicators to zero, re-forecasting exports to isolate 2019–2021 U.S. measure impact
  • UK-India FTA Paths: Designed multiple scenarios (immediate liberalization, phased cuts, delayed implementation) with alternative tariff and price shocks

📌 Key Insights

  • GDP Sensitivity: Whisky exports showed 0.68 elasticity to partner-country GDP, confirming premium spirits as income-sensitive luxury goods
  • Tariff Impact: Under no-tariff counterfactual, UK whisky exports to U.S. would have been 14-22% higher during 2019-2021
  • FTA Opportunity: Front-loaded tariff reductions in UK-India FTA projected 31% export growth over 5 years vs. phased approaches
  • Exchange Rate Role: GBP–INR movements explained 18% of export variance, highlighting currency hedging opportunities

🛠️ Tech Stack

Python 3.10 (pandas, numpy, matplotlib, seaborn, statsmodels, xgboost, scikit-learn, plotly, Dash) with fully reproducible Jupyter workflow (cleaning, EDA, model selection, analysis, and dashboard notebooks).

Automated HR & Payroll Management System

🎯 Executive Summary

Built a desktop-based HR and payroll application in Python to centralize employee records, attendance, and salary calculation for small factories (<50 employees) currently relying on spreadsheets. The system combines a Tkinter GUI with an SQLite backend to reduce manual errors, enforce consistent pay rules, and provide a single source of truth for HR and payroll operations.

💼 The Business Problem

Small and medium manufacturing units often track hours, leave, and pay in separate Excel files or paper registers. This leads to:

  • Frequent miscalculations of monthly wages (especially when handling half days, absences, and holidays)
  • Duplicate or inconsistent employee records across files, increasing compliance risk
  • HR staff spending hours each month reconciling attendance and salary instead of focusing on hiring and retention
  • No audit trail - factory owners lack confidence in payroll accuracy and have no quick way to audit decisions if disputes arise

💡 The "So What?" - Business Impact

  • Risk Reduction: By consolidating employee data, attendance logs, and salary calculations into a single GUI-driven application with ACID-compliant SQLite backend, we eliminated payroll dispute risk and compliance exposure from fragmented spreadsheets.
  • Operational Time Savings: Cuts payroll processing time from ~8 hours to <45 minutes by automating attendance-based salary calculations (present days, half days, absences) into a single "Calculate Salary" flow.
  • Improved Governance: Structured employee attributes (Aadhar, PAN, emergency contacts, blood group) and role-based workflows create an audit trail for regulatory inspections.
  • Scalability: System handles 50+ employees with zero marginal labor cost increase, enabling growth without proportional HR headcount.

🔧 Data & Methodology

Tech Stack

  • Python (Tkinter, tkcalendar): Multi-tab desktop interface with date pickers
  • SQLite: ACID-compliant relational backend (attendancewagesystem.db)
  • SQLAlchemy: ORM abstraction for future expansion (backups, exports, ML helpers)

Data Model

The core schema separates master data from transactional data:

  • Employee: employeeid (PK), name, designation, base salary, joining date, address, Aadhar, voter ID, PAN, mobile, emergency contact, blood group, part-time flag
  • MonthlyAttendance: employeeid, date, status (Present/Half Day/Absent), holidaycount
  • HolidayTracking: employeeid, month, holidaystaken

Application Workflow

Three primary workflows in separate tabs:

  1. Employee Management: Add new employees with full demographic and compliance details. Display Treeview of all employees with key fields (ID, Name, Designation, Base Salary).
  2. Attendance Management: HR selects employee from dropdown and records daily status on chosen date. Records written to MonthlyAttendance with scrollable history.
  3. Salary Management: HR selects employee and date range. System retrieves base salary, counts present days, and calculates salary using business rule: baseSalary / 30 × effectiveWorkingDays

Business Rules Implemented

  • Attendance Encoding: Present = 1.0, Half Day = 0.5, Absent = 0 (stored per date per employee)
  • Salary Calculation: Monthly salary = baseSalary / 30 × effectiveWorkingDays (leveraging attendance data vs. static assumptions)
  • Data Quality Controls: Input validation (non-empty key fields), controlled vocabularies via radio buttons to avoid free-text errors

📌 Key Insights

  • Time Savings: Automated salary calculation reduced monthly payroll processing from ~8 hours to <45 minutes for 50-employee factory
  • Data Integrity: Centralizing employee data eliminated duplicate records and ensured 100% consistency in compliance fields (Aadhar, PAN)
  • Audit Readiness: Structured attendance schema enabled quick generation of absenteeism reports for management review
  • Scalability: System architecture supports growth to 100+ employees without performance degradation
