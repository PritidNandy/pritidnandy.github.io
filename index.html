Balaram Mullick & Radharaman Mullick
Business Operations Executive
Apr 2021 – Mar 2024 · Kolkata, India
SDLC ERP AWS Python · SQL React

The Chaos

Inventory and raw material godowns were managed via physical paper ledgers across 15+ retail sites. No audit trail, no real-time stock visibility, and massive margin leakage from "invisible" waste. Production and sales had no data link — what was made versus what was sold was anyone's guess.

The Transformation

Architected a cloud-based ERP (AWS, React, Python, SQL) from scratch. Automated reporting saved 10+ hours/month. Linking production ERP to POS drove a 5% gross profit uplift. A QR card ordering system replaced handwritten tickets, boosting customer engagement by 12%. Waste fell 18%.

  • The inventory problem was physical chaos. Embedded with production staff, mapped the full data flow from purchase order through factory transfer to final sale, ensuring full auditability in the ERP.
  • Reporting consumed time meant for decisions. Custom analytics platform automated recurring reporting — surfacing waste patterns hidden by manual processes.
  • Production and sales operated in isolation. Scoped and delivered the ERP–POS integration across 15+ sites — eliminating manual reconciliation and driving gross profit uplift.
  • Billing queues were costing sales. Redesigned customer journey with a QR card ordering flow. Engagement rose 12% as browsing time increased.
  • Directed legacy database cleanup and migration, and managed live deployment on AWS, establishing a post-deployment support framework.
Girish Chandra Dey & Nakur Chandra Nandy
Business Account Executive
Apr 2019 – Present · Kolkata, India
Forecasting Procurement Payroll Systems

The Chaos

Manual payroll errors were frequent. Supplier invoices rarely matched negotiated rates, causing silent contractual drift and lost margin. Procurement decisions were reactive — made on instinct, not data — with no benchmarking against live market rates.

The Transformation

A market-rate benchmarking system held vendors accountable — delivering a 9% reduction in raw material costs plus a further 2% in recurring savings through tightened controls. A rules-based payroll engine eliminated bonus errors entirely.

  • Supplier invoices were not matching rates. Cross-referenced purchase invoices against agreed contract terms, restoring contract discipline and defending margins.
  • Procurement was reactive. Structured vendor benchmarking compared live market rates before each purchase cycle.
  • Payroll was manual and error-prone. Digitised the system with a rules-based engine that automatically calculated leave allowances and festival-duty bonuses across seasonal peaks.
  • Built daily demand forecasts synthesising weather patterns, historical YoY growth, and trends — optimising fresh production inventory.
