Business Operations Executive

Balaram Mullick & Radharaman Mullick

Apr 2021 – Mar 2024 · Kolkata, India

SDLC ERP AWS Python · SQL React

The Chaos

Inventory and raw material godowns were managed via physical paper ledgers across 15+ retail sites. No audit trail, no real-time stock visibility, and massive margin leakage from "invisible" waste. Production and sales had no data link — what was made versus what was sold was anyone's guess.

The Transformation

Architected a cloud-based ERP (AWS, React, Python, SQL) from scratch. Automated reporting saved 10+ hours/month. Linking production ERP to POS drove a 5% gross profit uplift. A QR card ordering system replaced handwritten tickets, boosting customer engagement by 12%. Waste fell 18%.

  • The inventory problem was physical chaos. I embedded with production staff, mapped the full data flow from purchase order through factory transfer to final sale, then collaborated with the developer to encode it into the ERP — delivering real-time inventory tracking with full auditability.
  • Reporting consumed time that should have been spent on decisions. A custom ERP-linked analytics platform in Python, SQL, and React automated recurring reporting — saving 10+ hours per month and surfacing waste patterns that manual processes had hidden.
  • Production and sales operated in isolation. I scoped and delivered the ERP–POS integration across 15+ sites — eliminating manual reconciliation, removing costly per-machine licensing in favour of per-outlet billing, and driving a 5% gross profit uplift.
  • Billing queues were costing sales. I redesigned the customer journey with a QR card–based ordering flow — customers ordered at any counter, settled once at the till. Customer engagement rose 12% as browsing time in front of product counters increased.
  • Architected data flows for multi-department and multi-factory production, mapping inventory movement by category to ensure accurate ingestion and system scalability.
  • Directed legacy HR and production database cleanup and migration, standardising data into auditable formats before go-live.
  • Led UAT and bug verification, then managed live deployment of real-time dashboards on AWS — accessible via web frontend to decentralised teams.
  • Established post-deployment support framework and ran end-user training sessions, driving high adoption and iterating on features from continuous feedback.
  • Advised on data governance and retention policy, balancing compliance requirements with database performance to maintain a lean, high-speed environment.

"I didn't just build software — I interviewed the floor team to find out why physical books were failing them, then mapped every department's journey to ensure 100% adoption."

Business Account Executive

Girish Chandra Dey & Nakur Chandra Nandy

Apr 2019 – Present · Kolkata, India

Forecasting Procurement Payroll Systems

The Chaos

Manual payroll errors were frequent. Supplier invoices rarely matched negotiated rates, causing silent contractual drift and lost margin. Procurement decisions were reactive — made on instinct, not data — with no benchmarking against live market rates.

The Transformation

A market-rate benchmarking system held vendors accountable — delivering a 9% reduction in raw material costs plus a further 2% in recurring savings through tightened invoice controls. A rules-based payroll engine eliminated festival-bonus errors entirely.

  • Supplier invoices were not matching negotiated rates. By cross-referencing purchase invoices against agreed contract terms — including signature verification — I closed a recurring vendor discrepancy gap, restoring contract discipline and protecting margins.
  • Procurement was reactive, not strategic. A structured vendor benchmarking process compared live market rates before each purchase cycle — driving a 9% reduction in raw material costs, with enhanced vendor protocols securing a further 2% in recurring savings.
  • Payroll was manual and error-prone. I digitised the system with a rules-based engine that automatically calculated leave allowances, excess leave, and festival-duty bonus eligibility across seasonal peaks — eliminating administrative errors.
  • Built daily demand forecasts synthesising weather patterns, historical YoY growth, and week-over-week trends — optimising fresh production inventory and reducing both overstock and stockout events.
  • Analysed daily operational datasets to identify discrepancies, informing cost-reduction strategies that fed directly into pricing models and product design decisions.
  • Spearheaded the launch of 5+ new product varieties by integrating market analysis with customer feedback, delivering measurable gross profit lifts.
